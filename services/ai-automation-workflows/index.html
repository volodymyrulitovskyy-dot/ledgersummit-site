The Problem

Your Finance Processes Are a Chain of Manual Hand-offs

Think about your month-end close. It starts with someone downloading bank statements. Then someone else exports data from the ERP. A third person matches them in Excel. Errors get caught (or don't), corrections are made, and the whole thing moves to the next step. Each hand-off is a potential break-point: files get lost, naming conventions change, someone is on vacation, or the macro breaks.

The real cost isn't the time each step takes - it's the waiting between steps. A 10-day close isn't 10 days of work; it's 3 days of actual work spread across 10 days because of sequential hand-offs, review bottlenecks, and error corrections.

AI workflow automation eliminates the hand-offs. Data flows automatically from source to destination. Matching happens in real time. Exceptions are flagged and routed instantly. Your team focuses on the 5% that needs human judgment - not the 95% that doesn't.

Workflow Anatomy

What every Ledger Summit workflow contains

Seven elements, defined up front, documented in the runbook, logged on every run.

  1. 1. TriggerSchedule, webhook, file drop, or upstream completion event.
  2. 2. Source systemERP, bank, payroll, billing, CRM — pulled via API, SFTP, or feed.
  3. 3. AI stepExtract, classify, match, allocate, or generate — with a confidence score.
  4. 4. ValidationTie-out checks, totals, duplicate detection, business-rule guards.
  5. 5. ApprovalNamed approver above thresholds; auto-approve below with full traceability.
  6. 6. OutputJE post, payment, report, dashboard refresh, or downstream system update.
  7. 7. Audit logInputs, model version, prompts, outputs, approvers — immutable record.

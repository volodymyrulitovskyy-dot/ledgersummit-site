What it is

A commercial loan amortization schedule builder breaks each payment into principal and interest, tracks the running balance, and supports lender packages, debt roll-forwards, and GL entries.

Who it is for

Controllers, bookkeepers, CFOs, and finance teams recording loan payments in the general ledger.

What matters most

Loan amount, annual interest rate, and term in months determine the payment amount and the interest/principal split for each period.

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually discuss first once the calculation or analysis is visible.

Loan amount

Confirm the principal amount matches the executed loan agreement, net of any origination fees or discounts.

Interest rate

Verify the nominal annual rate matches the loan agreement. For variable-rate loans, use the current rate for the schedule.

Term

Confirm the term in months reflects the full repayment period, including any balloon payment or refinancing assumptions.

Payment calculation

Verify the calculated monthly payment matches the lender's amortization schedule or payment notice.

Balance tracking

Check that the ending balance after the final payment is zero, confirming the schedule fully amortizes the loan.

Total interest

Review the total interest cost over the life of the loan against budget or forecast assumptions.

FAQ

Commercial Loan Amortization Schedule Builder questions, answered directly

Written in short form so searchers can get a clear answer without digging through generic product copy.

A loan amortization schedule is a month-by-month table showing how each payment is split between interest expense and principal reduction, along with the running loan balance after each payment.

Interest is calculated on the outstanding principal balance. As each payment reduces the balance, the next period's interest charge is smaller. The principal portion of each payment grows as the interest portion shrinks.

The monthly payment is calculated using the standard annuity formula: payment equals principal times the monthly rate divided by one minus one plus the monthly rate raised to the negative number of payments.

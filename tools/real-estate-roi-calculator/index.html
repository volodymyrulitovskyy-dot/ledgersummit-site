Guide Below The Tool

How to use the rental property roi calculator well

What it is

A rental property ROI calculator divides annual cash flow or total return by total cash invested so landlords can compare financed deals on a like-for-like basis.

Who it is for

Real estate investors, property managers, CPAs, and anyone evaluating whether a rental property acquisition meets their return requirements.

What matters most

Vacancy rate, operating expense accuracy, and the debt service amount have the largest impact on cash-on-cash return for leveraged properties.

Workflow

Four practical steps

1
Enter the full acquisition cost, not just the purchase price.

Include closing costs and renovation expenses in total investment for an accurate cash-on-cash denominator.

2
Use market vacancy, not optimistic occupancy.

A 5-10% vacancy rate is realistic for most single-family and small multifamily properties in stable markets.

3
Include all operating expenses before debt service.

Property tax, insurance, maintenance, property management fees, and reserves reduce NOI and must be included.

4
Compare cash-on-cash return against your target hurdle rate.

Use appreciation as context, not as a primary return justification - cash flow is what pays the mortgage.

Operating expense completeness

Confirm all expenses are included: property management (8-12% of rent is common), repairs, insurance, taxes, HOA if applicable.

CapEx reserves

Include a capital reserve allowance (1-2% of property value annually) for roof, HVAC, and major system replacement.

Debt service accuracy

Use actual PITI (principal, interest, taxes, insurance) if escrow is included in the mortgage payment.

Appreciation assumptions

Historical appreciation averages 3-4% nationally but varies significantly by market. Use conservative estimates.

Tax impact

This tool calculates pre-tax returns. Depreciation deductions, passive loss rules, and capital gains treatment affect after-tax returns significantly.

Sensitivity analysis

Run the tool with higher vacancy and lower rent to stress-test the deal before committing capital.

Rental Property ROI Calculator questions, answered directly

Real estate ROI is calculated by dividing annual net income (or total return including appreciation) by total cash invested, which includes down payment, closing costs, and renovation expenses.

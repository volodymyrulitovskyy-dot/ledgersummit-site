Guide Below The Tool

How to use the accounts receivable roll-forward tool well

This section is written for searchers, answer engines, and busy finance teams: direct definitions, practical steps, and concrete follow-up guidance.

What it is

An accounts receivable roll-forward reconciles beginning AR to ending AR through sales, collections, write-offs, and adjustments, creating the movement schedule auditors and controllers rely on during close.

Who it is for

Controllers, AR managers, auditors, FP&A analysts, and revenue accountants who need to explain the change in receivables between reporting periods.

What matters most

Accurate beginning balances, complete sales and collection records, properly documented write-offs, and clear adjustment descriptions are essential for a clean AR reconciliation.

Workflow

Four practical steps

Use the tool as a fast decision layer. The goal is to move from raw assumptions to a usable finance answer before you open a larger model.

1
Enter beginning AR balances.

Start with the prior period ending receivables for each customer or category.

2

3

Verify that ending AR reconciles to the sub-ledger and identify the largest movements by customer.

4
Export for audit or reporting.

Use the schedule in workpapers, AR aging packages, or management reporting decks.

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually discuss first once the calculation or analysis is visible.

Beginning balance tie-out

Confirm beginning AR matches the prior period ending balance from the sub-ledger or prior audit workpaper.

Revenue completeness

Verify that all invoiced sales for the period are captured and tie to the revenue recognition schedule.

Collection application

Check that cash receipts are applied to the correct customer invoices and reflected in the roll-forward.

Write-off authorization

Confirm that write-offs follow the company approval policy and are supported by documentation of uncollectibility.

Credit memo adjustments

Validate that adjustments such as returns, discounts, and credits are properly approved and recorded.

Aging concentration

Assess whether any single customer or aging bucket represents an outsized portion of ending AR, indicating collection risk.

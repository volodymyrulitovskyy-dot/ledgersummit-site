Guide Below The Tool

How to use the SAFE note conversion calculator well

This section is written for searchers, answer engines, and busy startup teams: direct definitions, practical steps, and concrete follow-up guidance.

What it is

A SAFE conversion calculator determines the number of shares issued when a SAFE converts at a priced round by comparing the valuation cap and discount to find the lower effective price.

Who it is for

Founders, startup CFOs, angel investors, and startup attorneys modeling SAFE conversions.

What matters most

SAFE investment amount, valuation cap, discount percentage, priced round PPS, and existing shares outstanding.

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually discuss first once the conversion calculation is visible.

SAFE type is identified

Confirm whether the SAFE is pre-money or post-money, as the conversion math differs significantly.

Valuation cap is correct

Verify the cap matches the signed SAFE agreement, not a term sheet draft.

Discount rate is accurate

Confirm the discount percentage and whether it applies to the round PPS or the pre-money valuation.

Round PPS is final

Use the final priced round PPS, not an indicative or estimated price.

Existing shares are fully diluted

Include all options, warrants, and other convertible instruments in the share count.

MFN clause is considered

If the SAFE has a Most Favored Nation clause, check whether later SAFEs grant better terms.

Guide Below The Tool

How to estimate your side hustle tax bill

Key concepts, practical steps, and guidance for managing side hustle tax obligations.

What it is

A side hustle tax calculator determines the total tax burden on self-employment income when combined with W-2 wages. It calculates self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35% of net profit), incremental federal income tax from bracket stacking, state income tax, and quarterly estimated payment amounts.

Who it is for

Anyone with both W-2 employment and side income from freelancing, gig work, consulting, or a small business. Also useful for people considering starting a side hustle who want to understand the tax implications before committing.

What matters most

Your W-2 salary determines your starting tax bracket, and side hustle net profit stacks on top. Self-employment tax at 15.3% is separate from income tax and is the biggest surprise for most side hustlers. Business expenses reduce both SE tax and income tax.

Review Checklist

What to validate first

Key assumptions that affect your side hustle tax calculation.

Gross vs net income

Make sure you are entering gross side hustle income and listing expenses separately. The calculator subtracts expenses to determine net profit for tax purposes.

All business expenses captured

Every legitimate deduction reduces your SE tax and income tax. Track home office, mileage, equipment, software, internet, phone, and professional development.

Correct W-2 salary

Your W-2 income determines your starting tax bracket. Side hustle income stacks on top, so an incorrect W-2 figure will skew the bracket stacking calculation.

Filing status accuracy

Filing status affects bracket widths and standard deduction amounts. Married filing jointly has wider brackets than single, which changes the incremental tax rate.

State tax rate

State income tax rates vary widely. Some states have no income tax, while others exceed 10%. Use your actual state rate for an accurate total tax estimate.

Quarterly payment timing

IRS quarterly due dates are April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15. Missing these dates triggers underpayment penalties even if you pay in full at filing.

FAQ

Side hustle tax questions, answered directly

Short answers for searchers and answer engines.

Side hustle income is subject to self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35% of net profit for Social Security and Medicare) plus federal and state income tax. The income stacks on top of any W-2 salary, so it is taxed at your highest marginal bracket.

Self-employment tax covers both the employer and employee portions of Social Security (12.4%) and Medicare (2.9%), totaling 15.3%. It applies to 92.35% of net self-employment income. You can deduct half of SE tax from your AGI.

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes at filing, the IRS requires quarterly estimated payments. Due dates are April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15.

Guide Below The Tool

How to use the FICA tax calculator well

Key concepts, practical steps, and guidance for calculating employment taxes accurately.

What it is

A FICA tax calculator computes Social Security tax (6.2% up to the wage base) and Medicare tax (1.45% on all wages, plus 0.9% additional Medicare tax above the threshold) for both the employee and employer portions, factoring in year-to-date wages and filing status.

Who it is for

Employees who want to understand their paycheck deductions, payroll administrators verifying withholding amounts, small business owners calculating employer costs, and accountants reconciling quarterly 941 filings.

What matters most

The Social Security wage base ($184,500 for 2026) caps the 6.2% tax. Year-to-date wages determine how much of the current period is still taxable. The additional Medicare tax threshold depends on filing status, not just income level.

Review Checklist

What to validate first

Key details that affect FICA tax calculations.

Correct year-to-date wages

The most common FICA calculation error is using the wrong YTD wages. Pull from your most recent pay stub or payroll report to get an accurate number.

Pre-tax deduction treatment

Most Section 125 (cafeteria plan) deductions reduce wages subject to FICA. However, 401(k) contributions do not reduce FICA-taxable wages. Know which deductions apply.

Multiple employer wage base

If you work for multiple employers, each employer withholds Social Security up to the full wage base. You may be over-withheld and can claim a credit on your tax return.

Self-employment tax comparison

Self-employed individuals pay both halves of FICA (15.3%) as self-employment tax. If you have both W-2 and self-employment income, the wage base applies to combined earnings.

Additional Medicare tax withholding timing

Employers must withhold the additional 0.9% Medicare tax once wages exceed $200,000 in a calendar year, regardless of filing status. Adjustments happen on your tax return.

Annual wage base changes

The Social Security wage base increases most years based on national average wage growth. Verify you are using the correct year's wage base when calculating.

FAQ

FICA tax questions, answered directly

Short answers for searchers and answer engines.

FICA stands for the Federal Insurance Contributions Act. It combines Social Security tax (6.2%) and Medicare tax (1.45%) withheld from employee wages. Employers pay a matching amount, making the total rate 15.3% on wages up to the Social Security wage base.

The 2026 FICA rate is 7.65% for employees (6.2% Social Security + 1.45% Medicare) and 7.65% for employers, totaling 15.3%. Social Security applies to wages up to $184,500. Medicare applies to all wages, with an additional 0.9% above $200,000 for single filers.

The 2026 Social Security wage base is $184,500. This is the maximum earnings subject to the 6.2% Social Security tax. Wages above this amount are exempt from Social Security tax, though Medicare tax still applies.

An extra 0.9% Medicare tax applies to wages exceeding $200,000 (single), $250,000 (married filing jointly), or $125,000 (married filing separately). Only the employee pays this tax. The employer does not match it.

Both. Employees pay 7.65% and employers match 7.65%. The additional 0.9% Medicare tax is employee-only. Self-employed individuals pay both halves (15.3%) as self-employment tax, with a deduction for half.

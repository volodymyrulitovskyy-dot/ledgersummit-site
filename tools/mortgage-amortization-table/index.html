Why amortization schedules matter for real estate accounting

For rental properties, interest is deductible and principal is not. The amortization table is the source document for splitting each mortgage payment into its tax and accounting components.

Interest expense is deductible - principal paydown is notThe amortization table provides the interest deduction amount for Schedule E or the income statement each year.

Guide Below The Tool

How to use the investment property mortgage amortization table well

What it is

An investment property mortgage amortization table shows how each payment splits between interest and principal and how the loan balance declines over the holding period.

Who it is for

Real estate investors, CPAs, controllers, and property managers who need annual interest expense amounts for Schedule E preparation, debt roll-forward reconciliation, or cash flow analysis.

What matters most

The annual interest expense figure from the amortization table is the number that flows into the Schedule E deduction. Using the right rate and outstanding balance is critical for accuracy.

Workflow

Four practical steps

1
Enter the original loan amount, rate, and term.

Use the original loan amount, not the current outstanding balance, to generate the full schedule from origination.

2
Review the annual interest expense for the current tax year.

The interest amount in the current year row flows directly to Schedule E line 12 (mortgage interest paid).

3
Reconcile the year-end balance against the lender statement.

The end-of-year outstanding balance should match the principal balance on the annual mortgage statement from the lender.

4
Export the schedule for the debt roll-forward or tax workpaper.

The annual summary is export-ready for use in a debt roll-forward schedule, balance sheet reconciliation, or financial model.

Rate confirmation

Use the contractual note rate, not the APR. The APR includes fees that are not part of the monthly payment calculation.

Escrow separation

If the monthly payment includes escrow for property taxes and insurance, those are not part of the principal and interest amortization. The amortization table covers P&I only.

Lender statement reconciliation

Reconcile the year-end balance to the lender Form 1098 or year-end statement. Minor rounding differences may appear; investigate differences above $10.

Multiple loans

For properties with multiple mortgages (first and second), run a separate schedule for each loan and sum the interest for Schedule E.

Refinancing impact

Refinancing resets the amortization schedule. Create a new schedule from the refinance date using the new principal, rate, and term.

Prepayments

This schedule assumes no prepayments. If extra principal has been paid, the outstanding balance will differ from the schedule - reconcile against the actual lender statement.

FAQ

Investment Property Mortgage Amortization Table questions, answered directly

A mortgage amortization table shows the breakdown of each payment into principal and interest over the life of the loan, along with the outstanding balance after each payment.

The monthly payment is calculated using the formula P x r / (1 - (1+r)^-n), where P is the loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the total number of payments.

For rental property accounting, the interest portion of each mortgage payment is a deductible expense. The principal portion reduces the outstanding loan balance. The amortization table separates these two components each period.

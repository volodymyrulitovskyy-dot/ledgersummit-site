Guide Below The Tool

How to use the home office deduction calculator effectively

Key concepts, practical steps, and guidance for maximizing your home office deduction.

What it is

A home office deduction calculator compares the two IRS-approved methods for deducting home office expenses: the simplified method ($5 per square foot, up to $1,500) and the regular method (actual home expenses multiplied by business-use percentage). It shows which method produces the larger deduction for your specific home and office setup.

Who it is for

Self-employed individuals, freelancers, independent contractors, and sole proprietors who work from a dedicated home office space. Tax professionals preparing Schedule C for clients with home offices also benefit from quick method comparisons.

What matters most

Office square footage relative to total home size determines your business-use percentage. Total housing expenses determine the regular method deduction. If the regular method deduction exceeds $1,500, the extra record-keeping effort pays off. The only way to know is to calculate both.

Workflow

Four practical steps

Use this calculator to decide which method to use before tax filing, and to quantify the benefit of maintaining a qualifying home office.

If the regular method is only slightly larger, the simplified method may be worth the convenience. If it is significantly larger, the record-keeping effort is justified.

Review Checklist

What to validate first

Key assumptions that affect your home office deduction calculation.

Exclusive use requirement

The space must be used exclusively and regularly for business. A multi-purpose room does not qualify unless you can identify a specific, dedicated area.

Square footage accuracy

Measure your office space carefully. The business-use percentage is the foundation of the regular method calculation and affects every expense allocation.

Rent vs mortgage distinction

Renters deduct a portion of rent. Homeowners deduct a portion of mortgage interest (not principal), property taxes, and home depreciation. These are different calculations.

Direct vs indirect expenses

Expenses that benefit only the office (painting the office) are 100% deductible. Expenses that benefit the whole home (utilities) are deducted at the business-use percentage.

Depreciation recapture

If you claim home depreciation under the regular method and later sell your home, you may owe depreciation recapture tax. Factor this into your decision.

Income limitation

The home office deduction under the regular method cannot exceed your business net income. Unused deduction can carry forward to future years.

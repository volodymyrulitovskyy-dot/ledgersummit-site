Guide Below The Tool

How to analyze vendor spend in QuickBooks Online

This section explains what the vendor spend analyzer does, which inputs matter, and how to move from a raw vendor list to concentration risk awareness and negotiation leverage.

What it is

A vendor spend analyzer ranks your top vendors by AP spend, calculates each vendor's share of total spend, and flags concentration risk and negotiation opportunities.

Who it is for

Controllers, AP managers, bookkeepers, CFOs, and operators who want to understand where their money goes and which vendors deserve closer attention.

What matters most

Total AP spend for the period, the number of active vendors, and the spend amounts for your top vendors determine the quality of the analysis.

Workflow

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually investigate once vendor concentration data is visible.

Top-vendor share of total AP

If a single vendor exceeds 20 percent of total spend, the team should evaluate alternatives and contract terms.

Top-five cumulative percentage

When the top five vendors account for more than 60 percent of spend, concentration risk is elevated.

Vendor count versus spend distribution

A large vendor count with highly concentrated spend suggests tail-spend cleanup opportunities.

Contract renewal timing

High-spend vendors approaching renewal deserve proactive negotiation before auto-renewal kicks in.

Early-payment discount potential

Vendors with consistent high spend are strong candidates for 2/10 net 30 or similar early-pay terms.

Tail-spend consolidation

Small vendors providing similar services can often be consolidated to improve terms and reduce invoice volume.

FAQ

QBO Vendor Spend Analyzer questions, answered directly

Written in short form so searchers can get a clear answer without digging through generic product copy.

Vendor concentration risk measures how much of your total AP spend depends on a small number of suppliers. High concentration means disruption at one vendor can materially affect operations.

In most businesses, roughly 80 percent of AP spend flows to 20 percent of vendors. Identifying that top tier helps prioritize negotiation, contract review, and risk management.

Volume discount requests, early-payment discounts, multi-year contract terms, and competitive bidding are the most effective tactics once you know which vendors absorb the largest share of spend.

Tail spend is the long list of small vendors that individually look insignificant but collectively can represent 20 to 30 percent of total AP. Consolidating tail spend reduces invoice volume and improves terms.

Guide Below The Tool

How to use the foreign tax credit calculator well

This section is written for searchers, answer engines, and busy international tax teams: direct definitions, practical steps, and concrete follow-up guidance.

What it is

A foreign tax credit calculator computes the IRC Section 904 limitation by comparing foreign taxes paid to the FTC limit based on the ratio of foreign-source to worldwide income.

Who it is for

International tax directors, corporate tax teams, tax advisors, and controllers managing cross-border tax positions.

What matters most

Foreign-source income, worldwide taxable income, foreign taxes paid, and the US tax liability before credits are the main drivers of the FTC limitation.

Workflow

Four practical steps

Use the tool as a fast decision layer. The goal is to move from raw tax data to a sized FTC position before you finalize the provision or the return.

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually discuss first once the foreign tax credit calculation is visible.

Source of income rules

Confirm that income has been sourced correctly under the IRC rules before applying the limitation formula.

Basket allocation

Verify that income and taxes are allocated to the correct FTC basket (general vs passive) to avoid cross-crediting issues.

Creditable tax analysis

Ensure that foreign taxes meet the creditability requirements under Section 901 and the 2022 final regulations.

Expense allocation

Check that expenses have been properly allocated and apportioned to foreign-source income under Section 861.

Carryforward tracking

Track excess credits by year and basket to ensure proper utilization within the carryforward period.

Credit vs deduction election

Evaluate whether electing to deduct foreign taxes instead of crediting them produces a better overall result.

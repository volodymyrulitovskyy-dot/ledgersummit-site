Guide Below The Tool

How to use the cap rate calculator well

This section is written for searchers, answer engines, and busy real estate teams: direct definitions, practical steps, and concrete follow-up guidance.

What it is

A cap rate calculator divides net operating income by property value to produce the capitalization rate, the most common measure of commercial real estate yield.

Who it is for

Real estate investors, commercial brokers, acquisition analysts, property managers, lenders, and anyone evaluating whether a property's income justifies its price.

What matters most

Net operating income accuracy and the property value or asking price are the two inputs that drive the cap rate. Garbage NOI produces a garbage cap rate.

Workflow

Four practical steps

Use the tool as a fast decision layer. The goal is to move from raw property financials to a usable yield metric before you open a full underwriting model.

Review Checklist

What reviewers usually validate first

These are the areas teams usually discuss first once the cap rate calculation is visible.

NOI accuracy

Verify that operating expenses are complete and realistic. Understating expenses inflates NOI and makes the cap rate look better than reality.

Vacancy and collection loss

Confirm that the NOI already reflects a market vacancy factor. Pro-forma NOI with zero vacancy overstates income.

Capital expenditure reserves

Check whether the NOI deducts a reserve for roof, HVAC, and other capital items. Ignoring CapEx replacement inflates current yield.

Market cap rate comparison

Compare the calculated cap rate against recent comparable sales in the same submarket and property class.

Debt service coverage

Lenders typically require a DSCR of 1.20x or higher. A property with strong cap rate but weak DSCR may not qualify for financing.

Value-add versus stabilized analysis

Determine whether the NOI reflects current in-place income or a projected stabilized number. Cap rates on pro-forma NOI carry more execution risk.

Why migrate (and why now)

This client had been running on QuickBooks Online Advanced and the workarounds were starting to outweigh the benefits. They were spending 5 days a month on manual consolidation across their 3 entities, couldn't get usable project profitability, and were getting close to needing audit-ready financials for a debt refinance.

  • Manual multi-entity consolidation, 5+ days per month
  • No native project accounting; workaround in spreadsheets
  • QBO class structure exhausted; no real dimensional reporting
  • Audit-ready needs for debt refinance in 6 months
  • Annual revenue projected to hit $50M within 12 months

What we implemented in 90 days

A standard NetSuite implementation done by a controller's playbook: design the chart of accounts and dimensions before configuration, plan integrations early, parallel close in month 1, and document everything for the auditor.

  • NetSuite OneWorld with 3 subsidiaries and consolidated reporting
  • Chart of accounts with entity, department, and project dimensions
  • Project accounting (services delivery, billing, profitability)
  • AP automation via Bill, AR automation via NetSuite native
  • Integrations with payroll (Gusto), CRM (HubSpot), and bank feeds
  • Tool Box AI workflow layer for approvals, exception handling, evidence packs
  • Data migration with reconciled before/after totals

Controls, data, and risk management

  • Risk register with named owner and rollback plan per migration milestone
  • Trial balance reconciled to zero before cutover; documented and signed off
  • Parallel close month: every variance investigated and resolved before cutover
  • User roles and permissions designed for SOX-readiness (debt-refinance audit pending)
  • Tool Box AI evidence packs operating from close 1 forward

What we'd do the same (and what we'd change)

Same: spending the first 14 days on COA and dimension design. The integration map up front. The 15-day parallel close. Tool Box AI layer planned in scope.

Change: more aggressive vendor-master cleanup before migration (we did some, but ran into duplicates post-cutover). Earlier engagement with the auditor to walk through the new evidence pack design.

Frequently asked questions

Can a real NetSuite implementation actually finish in 90 days?

For this size and complexity (~$30M revenue, 3 entities, services delivery), yes — but only with disciplined scope and an experienced team. We say no to scope expansion during the implementation; new requests go on a post-go-live optimization list. Larger or more complex companies (5+ entities, manufacturing, regulated) typically run 120–180 days.

What was the cost ballpark?

We share specific pricing in scoping calls. For context: a $25–50M services company with 2–4 entities typically runs in the mid-six-figures all-in (NetSuite license, implementation, integrations, hypercare). We size to the actual scope, not a percentage of license.

Did you keep QuickBooks running for any period after cutover?

Read-only access for historical lookup, kept for 18 months post-cutover (until past the audit cycle). All current-period work moved to NetSuite at cutover; no dual-system bookkeeping.

How the workflow changed from manual effort to controlled automation

These pages now follow the same Ledger Summit story structure as the main site: context first, implementation detail second, measured results third, and controls throughout.

Before automation

Deferral schedules were maintained manually and often drifted from contract terms, causing corrections and delayed reporting.

  • Manual revenue and expense recognition calendars
  • Missed or duplicated reversal entries
  • Late identification of schedule mismatches

What Ledger Summit implemented

Ledger Summit implemented rules-based deferral scheduling tied to term logic, reversal timing, and approval checkpoints.

  • Automated schedule generation from source attributes
  • System checks for term length and recognition start/end
  • Exception routing for non-standard contract patterns

Implementation timeline

  • Week 1: map current deferral policy and data sources
  • Week 2: configure schedule and reversal logic
  • Week 3: parallel period validation
  • Week 4: production rollout and control attestation

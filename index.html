Quality control

Month-end close, done right.

Our proof point is the operating system underneath the service: current-state process mapping, controlled posting, and an independent reconciliation layer that checks raw source data against what landed in the books.

1

Stabilize the accounting foundation

We map the current close, identify cleanup gaps, and create a service plan around owners, deadlines, evidence, and recurring review points.

2

Use AI to automate the repeatable work

Bank recs, payroll tie-outs, inventory recs, accruals, deferrals, and fixed-asset schedules move into AI-assisted workflows with exception handling and human review.

3

Reconcile source data to posted books

Raw source records are matched to posted accounting entries so errors are caught before financial statements, forecasts, and management reports are issued.

4

Turn close into leadership reporting

The close output becomes a reporting pack, KPI review, and cash-flow forecast that leadership can actually use.

FAQ

Questions accounting-services buyers ask first.

What is included in fractional CFO services?

Fractional CFO work includes cash-flow forecasting, KPI review, board or lender reporting, budgeting, scenario planning, and finance guidance for operating decisions.

How is outsourced controller work different from bookkeeping?

Bookkeeping keeps records current. Controller work owns the close process, reviews financial statement quality, manages accruals and reconciliations, and turns the books into reliable reporting.

Do you replace our accounting system?

No. Ledger Summit works around your existing accounting stack while improving process discipline, close visibility, reconciliation, and reporting outputs.

How does AI automation fit into the service?

AI automation is the delivery layer, not the headline product. It accelerates month-end close, reduces manual workflow steps, and helps automate FP&A reporting while controller review and reconciliation keep the output reliable.

What should we prepare before a first service review?

Bring the current close calendar, recent financial statements, key spreadsheets, system list, recurring pain points, and the reporting questions leadership asks every month.

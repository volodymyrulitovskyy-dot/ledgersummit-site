Case study breakdown

How the workflow changed from manual effort to controlled automation

These pages now follow the same Ledger Summit story structure as the main site: context first, implementation detail second, measured results third, and controls throughout.

Before automation

Approvals were managed through email and chat, with inconsistent documentation standards across account owners and reviewers.

  • Approval history spread across inboxes and local files
  • Support documents attached inconsistently
  • Frequent last-minute follow-up before sign-off

What Ledger Summit implemented

Ledger Summit configured a role-based approval matrix with clear thresholds, required support checks, and full event logs for every decision.

  • Approval paths by journal type, account, and amount
  • Required evidence checklist before reviewer sign-off
  • Timestamped records for submissions, approvals, and rejections

Implementation timeline

  • Week 1: documented current SOPs and approval pain points
  • Week 2: defined approval roles, thresholds, and backup approvers
  • Week 3: set evidence rules and tested sample entries
  • Week 4: pilot with two close cycles
  • Week 5: full go-live and control reporting

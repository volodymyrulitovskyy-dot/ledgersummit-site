Accounting Close Automation Case Study

A multi-entity manufacturer standardized month-end close, accruals, deferrals, and fixed-asset schedules into one controlled process.

Client profile: Multi-entity manufacturing business, USD 32M revenue, 8-person accounting team.

12 to 7 daysTotal close cycle
54% lessManual close-prep effort
68% fewerPost-close correction entries

Before automation

The close process relied on separate trackers for close tasks, accruals, deferrals, and fixed assets. Rework increased during final review.

  • Month-end task status spread across spreadsheets and email
  • Accrual and deferral journals prepared with inconsistent templates
  • Fixed-asset updates and depreciation checks were delayed to late close

What Ledger Summit implemented

Ledger Summit built one coordinated close workflow with due dates, owner assignments, journal validation checks, and structured fixed-asset updates.

  • Unified close calendar and owner accountability by task
  • Standard accrual and deferral templates with approval requirements
  • Fixed-asset schedule update process tied to depreciation posting review

Implementation timeline

  • Week 1: documented close SOPs and identified delay points
  • Week 2: configured close calendar, ownership, and due dates
  • Week 3: built accrual and deferral journal controls
  • Week 4: set fixed-asset update and depreciation checks
  • Week 5: parallel run for one month-end cycle
  • Week 6: production cutover and KPI tracking
